BLOCKCHAIN

What is blockchain

Blockchain is an ever expanding digital ledger, called blocks, which are interconnected like a chain and locked and secured by means of cryptographic programing.

Blockchain is the backbone for Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies, recording all transactions in the public ledger.

However, due to its solid, untamperable record keeping capabilities, blockchain technology is taking over industries around the world.

how does it work

A blockchain is the structure of data that represents a financial ledger entry, or a record of a transaction. Each transaction is digitally signed to ensure its authenticity and that no one tampers with it, so the ledger itself and the existing transactions within it are assumed to be of high integrity.

Blockchain is a decentralized technology meaning, not only one has power, but it is distributed through a network of users. The distributed consensus model allows blockchain to keep a smooth distributed ledger without the need for central authority.

Security

Blockchai is difficult to hack because of two principles:

  • 1 - Cryptographic fingerprint
  • 2 - Consensus protocol (a process where multiple nodes on the network agree upon).

Blockchain technology uses methods of cryptography to store data in each “block”, each block is unique and has a digital fingerprint. The fingerprint AKA - HASH is a series of complex algorithms that once solved, become “proof of work” showing the all network agrees upon terms and conditions for a specific transaction.
